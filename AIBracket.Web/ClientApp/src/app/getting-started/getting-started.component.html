Getting Started With AI Bracket

AI Bracket is designed around writing bots to play simple games.
Based on what's going on in the game, you can design a simple AI to compete on a leaderboard for the highest score.

Creating a Bot

You can create a bot using any language. Once you are ready to test your bot, create an account and log in to access your profile. Select the new bot + and enter the required fields to recieve a private key for your bot.

Statistics for every bot any bot you own can be seen on your profile along with your match history.

Connecting a Bot

Connect to AIBracket.com:8000 and send a message BOT followed by your private key to get your bot started.
This will allow you to send and recieve data to the server about your match and your bot can process it.

***The API will require some information about your intentions, whether you are connecting for a single player game, versus game, or a tournament.


Game Events Documentation

Everything that happens in the game will be sent to you using an event system. Each game has its own unique events specified by an enumeration as the first section delimeted by a space. Events are sent to you at even intervals so sometimes there will be multiple updates. These updates are delimeted by an *.

Example: 3 25 2 10

In this example the 3 tell you what kind of event this is. In the case of Pacman this would mean it is an update telling that a dot was consumed. The Dot event has its own information that you need such as which dot, and how much to add to the score. This can be seen in the example where the 25 is the x coordinate of the dot, and the 2 is the y coordinate. The score is on the end as a 10.

  • A board reset event is represented as a 0 and is followed by Width and Height of the board.
    • Everytile is represented by a 0 for a wall, 1 for a blank, 2 for a dot, 3 for a fruit, 4 for a power up, and 5 for a portal.
    • Fruit will not be spawned any time the map is reset, so this can be ignored.
    • The list is from left to right, top to bottom.
  • A pacman position event is represented as a 1 and will be followed by Pacman's new X and Y position.
  • A ghost position event is represented as a 2 and will be followed by a 0 1 2 or 3 indicating which ghost is being updated, followed by the ghost's new X and Y, and two booleans indicating whether the ghost is dead and/or is vulnerable to dying.
  • All the following have an X and Y coordinate of the location and a positive value to add to the score (this can be ignored if you don't want to factor score into your bot)
    • A dot being consumed is represented as a 3
    • A fruit being consumed is represented as a 4
    • A power up being consumed is represented as a 5
  • A fruit being spawned is represented as a 6 and is followed by the X and Y coordinate of where it was spawned.
  • Pacman lives updates are represented as a 7 and are followed by the number of lives.
  • When a ghost dies or spawns it is represented as an 8 and is followed by a 0 1 2 or 3 indicating which ghost is affected, and a boolean indicating if it's a death.
  • The score event is shown at the beginning of the game or if a spectator joins and is indicated by a 9.
